Our Institutional Partners

These are some of our partners.

Deen Dayal Upadhyaya (DDU) college is a constituent and public college of the University of Delhi. DDU college has 1800 undergraduates and 110 postgraduate students.
The College is spread in an area of 7.7 acres which includes well-equipped laboratories, a library, wifi-enabled computer Centre and an auditorium for conducting cultural events and annual fests. There is also an air conditioned canteen for students and staff in the college. The college also has hostel facilities for students.
Deen Dayal Upadhyaya college was ranked 15th among Indian colleges by the National Institute ranking framework (NIRF) in 2020. Deen Dayal Upadhyaya college is affiliated with the university grants commission (UGC) and National Assessment and Accreditation Council (NAAC) with a Grade ‘B’ and CGPA of 2.63 on 4 Point Scale.

Rotaract club of Delhi janak is a non-profitable organisation, which work for the society in and out. The club is under Rotaract district 3011.
They bring professional leaders and youth in order to provide humanitarian service and to advance goodwill and peace around the world. They are a part of ROTARY INTERNATIONAL. The club was chartered on 28 October 2015 under the Rotary International District 3011. They are sponsored by the Rotary Club of Delhi Janak. They have been serving the society for the past Seven Years.Club Theme for Rota Year 2021-2022 is Together Towards tomorrow

Kamala Nehru College is one of the affiliated colleges of the University of Delhi offering UG courses under the faculties of Arts, Commerce and Science. The B.A. degree course is offered in 13 disciplines. The other UG courses include B.Com, and B.Sc. Mathematics Honours. At the PG level a single M.A course is offered in 6 specializations. Admission to any of the courses is based on merit or DUET scores.
The college was founded in 1964 and initially it functioned as a Government College for Women in Defence Colony with a student strength of 457 followed by a name change, Modern College for Women, with 1600 students. Later in 1975, the new college campus was shifted to Siri Fort Road and was renamed as Kamala Nehru College.

Maitreyi College is an all women’s college which is one of the affiliated colleges of the University of Delhi. The college offers 3 year undergraduate programs in the areas of Arts, Commerce and Science in various specialisations with a highly qualified and experienced faculty team. Some of the faculty members are trained in new Pedagogical Strategies involving innovative blended learning in the U.K.
Maitreyi College has achieved 35th position in India, according to the surveys conducted by the NIRF in the year 2020. The college has been well recognised as a star college by Department of Biotechnology (DBT) with conferring the star status to all four of its Science departments namely Botany, Zoology, Chemistry and Mathematics. The college has also been honoured Grade ‘A’ by the NAAC.

Miranda House, New Delhi is a top-ranking college for women affiliated under Delhi University. The college has an excellent team of faculties across all departments, especially, the Humanities and Social Science division which has 13 departments and is supposed to be the best among all other DU colleges. Miranda House offers courses in Science and Liberal Arts and admission is granted based on the merit obtained as per the cut-off percent decided by the University of Delhi.
Lady Edwina Mountbatten established Miranda House in 1948 with the purpose of promoting women’s higher education. NIRF 2021 has ranked it at the 1st position in India. The college has been accredited Grade A+ with 3.61 CGPA.

Pannalal Girdharlal Dayanand Anglo- Vedic College (PGDAV) is one of the oldest institutes of the University of Delhi. The college offers both undergraduate and post-graduate courses with different specializations across Arts, Commerce, and Science. Admission to the Undergraduate courses shall be merit-based and admission to Postgraduate courses shall be either merit-based or on the basis of rank secured in the entrance examination conducted by Delhi University (DU). The candidate should have cleared Higher Secondary Examination (12th) to be eligible for admission to UG courses and must hold Bachelor’s Degree to be eligible for PG courses.

Rajdhani College was formed in 1964 in Kirti Nagar, New Delhi by the Delhi administration and was referred to as Government College. Later it was renamed as Rajdhani College and affiliated to the Delhi University. The new college building has been functioning since 1977 in West Delhi on Mahatma Gandhi Marg, New Delhi. Rajdhani College has bagged NAAC accreditation of Grade B++ and an approval by UGC, MHRD.
